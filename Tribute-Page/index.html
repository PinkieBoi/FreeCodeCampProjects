Oscar Wilde

Author, Poet, Playwrite, Dandy

Life and Trials

  • 1854 Oscar Fingal O'Flahertie Wills Wilde is born on 16th October to Lord William Wilde & Lady Jane Wilde in Dublin, Ireland.
  • 1871 Wilde read Classics at Trinity College, Dublin under tutor Professor J. P. Mahaffey until 1874 where he won the Berkeley Gold Medal in Greek, the University's highest academic award.
  • 1874 Wilde won a demyship to Magdalen College, Oxford
  • 1878 Wilde won the Newdigate Prize for his poem 'Ravenna' and graduated with a double first in his B.A. of Classical Moderations and Literae Humaniores (Greats).
  • 1881 Published 'Poems' which collected, revised and and expanded his already published works.
  • 1882 Richard D'Oyly Carte invites Wilde to make a lecture tour of North America which lasted almost a year due to success
  • 1884 Wilde married Constance Lloyd at St. James's Church, Paddington
  • 1890 'The Picture of Dorian Gray' is published in Lippincott's Monthly Magazine to much critial disdain however, after heavy revision for book publication the next year, Robert McCrum of The Guardian newspaper deemed the work to be the 27th greatest novel ever written in English.
  • 1891 Lionel Johnson introduced Wilde to his cousin, Lord Alfred 'Bosie' Douglas who would soon become Wilde's lover.
  • 1892 First performance of 'Lady Windermere's Fan' at St. James's Theatre
  • 1893 A Woman of No Importance debuts. 'Salome' is first published in Paris and London but not performed until 1896
  • 1895 'The Importance of Being Earnest' debuts.
    The Marquess of Queensbury leaves his card at Wilde's club enscribed "For Oscar Wilde, posing sodomite" & Wilde unsuccessfully prosecutes for libel and is finally arrested for gross indecency on 6th April and sentenced to 2 years hard labour served first in Newgat Prison & later Pentonville, Wandsworth and Reading Goal Prisons to which he later remarked that "If this is the way Queen Victoria treats her prisoners, she doesn't deserve to have any.".
  • 1897 Wilde is released from prison, spending his final years impoverished and in exile in Paris under the alias of Sebastian Melmoth
